MiteOut

Founder & CTO

Type PCB Design, Embedded Systems, Entrepreneurship
Status Ongoing
Year 2023 – Present
Stack Hardware / CAD / Software / Entrepreneurship

Overview

MiteOut is a precision agriculture startup building autonomous treatment hardware for varroa mite infestations in domestic beehives. As Founder and CTO, I engineered three custom PCB iterations in KiCad integrating 12+ sensors — weight, current/voltage, temperature, humidity, RTC, and solar/battery management — alongside 3 DC motors for automated miticide dosing.

Firmware is written in C++ on an ESP32 with Wi-Fi, enabling wireless data logging of hive health metrics. Embedded acquisition systems and control algorithms monitor hive conditions continuously and trigger treatment autonomously, removing the guesswork and labor burden from beekeepers.

The company has raised $8,000+ in funding through national competitions: Hult Prize (National Finalist), Dartmouth Social Blueprint Challenge (Winner), Siena College Business Plan Competition (Winner), and Thomas Edison Pitch Contest (2nd Place).

Process

The project began with identifying a critical need in beekeeping — varroa mite infestations are the leading cause of domestic honeybee colony collapse. Extensive beekeeper interviews defined the core pain points: treatment timing is difficult to judge, application is labor-intensive, and most hobbyists lack the tools to monitor hive health quantitatively.

Multiple product iterations followed, from breadboarded prototypes to custom-fabricated PCBs, before converging on the current MVP. Alongside hardware development, competition pitching served as both a funding mechanism and a forcing function for refining the product story. Development and deployment are ongoing.
