Overview

Manufacturing Engineering Intern at Philips Healthcare's largest facility and MRI manufacturing plant in Latham, NY. Responsible for automating and optimizing production tasks across the factory floor, with projects spanning mechanical fixture design, automated welding, and thermal process optimization.

Designed and optimized an automated welding device in Onshape, programmed in C++ (Arduino) with pneumatics, relay control, an industrial stepper driver, and a linear actuator — reducing welding cycle time by 90%. Separately designed and 3D-printed alignment fixtures that cut rework and assembly time for operators by 50%+. Implemented an optimized cooling algorithm in C++ with Excel simulation, decreasing per-unit cooldown time by 12%. All projects were presented in engineering reviews and adopted into production.

Process

Began each project by working directly with floor operators to identify where time was being lost and where error rates were highest. Physical and electrical prototypes were built quickly and iterated on based on operator feedback — tolerances, ergonomics, and workflow fit all drove revisions. Alongside the hardware work, each device required internal paperwork and certifications before it could be approved for the factory floor, which meant coordinating with engineers across departments. Progress was communicated through frequent engineering design reviews.
