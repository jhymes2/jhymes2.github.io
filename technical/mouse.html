Micromouse

Type Electrical Design, PCB Layout, Embedded Programming
Status Complete
Year 2024 – 2025
Stack Hardware / CAD / Software

Overview

Designed 10+ custom STM32-based micromouse PCBs for Dartmouth graduate classes, replacing bulky development kits to cut costs and tailor hardware to course requirements. The board integrates dual motors, encoders, an IMU, distance sensors, and Wi-Fi — all on a compact footprint purpose-built for maze-solving robotics.

Integrated and debugged PWM, SPI, I2C, and UART communication across all peripherals using oscilloscope, multimeter, and STM32CubeIDE. The platform enabled graduate students to program 3–5 micromouse robots to autonomously map mazes via Wi-Fi coordination.

Components were specified and PCB layouts optimized in KiCad for compactness and manufacturing reliability.

Process

Began by working with students and the course instructor to understand the constraints and failure modes of the existing mice, then produced a requirements list from that audit. Electrical components were sourced individually and bench-tested to confirm they met spec before committing to layout. The PCB was designed in KiCad around those validated components, followed by firmware development to bring all peripherals under unified software control.
